BRAND STORY 品牌故事

Inspired by Zen Eastern philosophy, our brand embodies a spirit of flow--unbound, energising, and intuitively alive.

It moves with purpose yet without resistance, creating an experience that feels both effortless and deeply considered.

WORDMARK

Our wordmark is always set small. It needs room to breathe, so keep whitespace generous.

Use it in black or white only or in Golden Yellow. Use a deboss it for an ultra-premium finish on packaging.

ART DIRECTION

A world where everything feels alive and continuously unfolding. Moments captured mid-flow. Dynamic and unexpected compositions that feel spontaneous.

Filled with energy, photography feel vivid and bold using slight blur and flowing forms.

High contrast and layered colour overlays intensify the sense of energy.

TYPOGRAPHY

Our brand typeface is Martina Plantijn Light. For small headings and buttons we use Greed Condensed Bold. Type is always set small and surrounded by plenty of whitespace.

COLOUR

Inspired by the flow of chakra energy, our colour palette is built as a continuous spectrum of feeling - from grounding balance to expressive vitality.

CORE COLOUR PALETTE

The core palette influences key brand elements, creating a consistent and recognisable foundation across all touch points.

COTTON

Used for base, backgrounds and to create breathing space for other elements.

INK

Used only for text: including motif, signature and body copy.

UMBER

Secondary base, used for bolder expression: for example campaign, social media and spatial design.

GOLDEN YELLOW

Leading brand colour, used for key brand moments, highlights, signature details, tags & labels.

Never used for motif or signature.

SECONDARY COLOUR PALETTE

The secondary palette brings energy and expression into the brand system. Inspired by materials, textures and cultural references, it can be used more fluidly.

USE

It is mainly used as accent colour for typography, seasonal collaborations, and editorial highlights; helping create contrast and draw attention.

In campaigns, social media, and spatial design, the palette can be used more boldly through strong backgrounds and larger colour moments.
